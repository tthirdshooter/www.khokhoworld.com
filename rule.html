History of the Game

Kho Kho is one of the oldest traditional tag sports in India. Its origins are difficult to trace, but many believe it is a modified form of 'Run Chase', which in its ancient form was played on chariots called Rathera.

Ancient India

Mentions of sports similar to Kho Kho found in the Mahabharata, specifically the 'Chakravyuha' tactics which mirror the defensive patterns used today.

1914

The first set of modern Kho Kho rules was framed by the Deccan Gymkhana Club of Pune.

1959

The Kho Kho Federation of India (KKFI) was formed, and the first National Championship was held in Vijayawada.

1987

Kho Kho was included as a demonstration sport in the SAF Games in Kolkata.

2022-Present

The launch of Ultimate Kho Kho brought the sport to the professional mat with league-based formats and television broadcasting.

Field Specifications

Modern Kho Kho is played on a rectangular court, traditionally mud but now high-quality synthetic mats in professional leagues.

Standard Dimensions: 27 Meters (Length) x 16 Meters (Width)
  • Free Zone: The area outside the poles (1.5m x 16m).
  • Poles: Two strong wooden/fiber posts fixed at the ends of the central lane.
  • Sitting Blocks: 8 squares in the central lane where chasers sit facing opposite directions.
